# Engineering Career Progression Path

## Overview
This document outlines the typical career progression for software engineers, from entry-level to senior technical leadership positions.

## Individual Contributor Track

### Junior Software Engineer (0-2 years)
- **Focus**: Learning fundamentals, writing quality code, fixing bugs
- **Scope**: Individual tasks and small features
- **Skills**: Programming basics, version control, testing
- **Next Step**: Software Engineer

### Software Engineer (2-4 years)
- **Focus**: Independent feature development, code reviews
- **Scope**: Complete features and modules
- **Skills**: System design basics, debugging, collaboration
- **Next Step**: Senior Software Engineer

### Senior Software Engineer (4-8 years)
- **Focus**: Technical leadership, mentoring, complex problems
- **Scope**: Large features, cross-team projects
- **Skills**: Architecture, optimization, mentorship
- **Next Steps**: Staff Engineer or Engineering Manager

### Staff Engineer (8-12 years)
- **Focus**: Technical strategy, cross-team architecture
- **Scope**: Multiple teams and systems
- **Skills**: Distributed systems, technical influence, strategic thinking
- **Next Step**: Senior Staff Engineer

### Senior Staff Engineer (10-15 years)
- **Focus**: Organization-wide technical initiatives
- **Scope**: Company-wide impact
- **Skills**: Technical vision, executive communication, innovation
- **Next Step**: Principal Engineer

### Principal Engineer (12+ years)
- **Focus**: Industry-leading expertise, technical direction
- **Scope**: Company and industry influence
- **Skills**: Thought leadership, strategic partnerships, research
- **Next Step**: Distinguished Engineer

### Distinguished Engineer (15+ years)
- **Focus**: Industry recognition, groundbreaking work
- **Scope**: Industry-wide impact
- **Skills**: Innovation, patents, public speaking, standards definition

## Management Track

### Tech Lead (4-6 years)
- **Focus**: Technical guidance, team coordination
- **Scope**: Single team technical decisions
- **Skills**: Technical leadership, project planning, mentoring
- **Next Step**: Engineering Manager

### Engineering Manager (5-8 years)
- **Focus**: People management, team delivery
- **Scope**: 5-10 engineers
- **Skills**: People management, hiring, performance reviews
- **Next Step**: Senior Engineering Manager

### Senior Engineering Manager (8-12 years)
- **Focus**: Multiple team management, strategic planning
- **Scope**: 10-20 engineers
- **Skills**: Organization building, budget management, stakeholder management
- **Next Step**: Engineering Director

### Engineering Director (10-15 years)
- **Focus**: Department leadership, technical strategy
- **Scope**: 20-50 engineers across multiple teams
- **Skills**: Strategic planning, executive communication, organizational design
- **Next Step**: VP of Engineering

### VP of Engineering (12+ years)
- **Focus**: Engineering organization leadership
- **Scope**: Entire engineering department
- **Skills**: Executive leadership, business strategy, culture building
- **Next Step**: CTO

### CTO (15+ years)
- **Focus**: Technology vision and strategy
- **Scope**: Company-wide technology decisions
- **Skills**: Business leadership, board communication, industry influence

## Transition Points

### From IC to Management
- **When**: After demonstrating technical leadership and interest in people management
- **Preparation**: Lead projects, mentor juniors, shadow managers
- **Considerations**: Different skill set, less coding, more meetings

### From Management to IC
- **When**: Desire to focus on technical work
- **Options**: Staff+ Engineer, Architect, Technical Specialist
- **Considerations**: Compensation adjustment, scope change

## Key Success Factors

### For IC Track
- Deep technical expertise
- Innovation and problem-solving
- Technical influence without authority
- Continuous learning
- Strong communication skills

### For Management Track
- People development skills
- Strategic thinking
- Business acumen
- Emotional intelligence
- Decision-making abilities

## Compensation Progression
- Each level typically includes 15-30% salary increase
- Equity grants increase significantly at senior levels
- Management and IC tracks have equivalent compensation at same levels

## Timeline Variations
- Fast Track: Exceptional performers may progress 30-50% faster
- Industry Switch: May require stepping back 1 level
- Advanced Degrees: May start 1 level higher
- Startup vs Enterprise: Different pace and expectations

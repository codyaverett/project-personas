# Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

## Overview
The CFO manages the financial actions of a company, including tracking cash flow, financial planning, analyzing the company's financial strengths and weaknesses, and proposing strategic directions.

## Key Responsibilities
- Oversee all financial operations and reporting
- Develop financial strategy and long-term business plans
- Manage cash flow, investments, and capital structure
- Lead budgeting, forecasting, and financial analysis
- Ensure compliance with financial regulations and standards
- Manage relationships with investors, banks, and auditors
- Oversee accounting, tax, treasury, and audit functions
- Identify and manage financial risks
- Support M&A activities and due diligence
- Present financial updates to board and executives

## Required Skills
- Advanced financial and accounting expertise
- Strategic planning and analysis
- Leadership and team management
- Regulatory compliance knowledge
- Risk management
- Communication and presentation skills
- Business partnering
- Technology and systems knowledge
- Negotiation skills
- Data analysis and interpretation

## Reporting Structure
- Reports to: CEO
- Direct Reports: VP of Finance, Controller, Treasurer, Head of FP&A, Head of Tax

## Success Metrics
- Accurate financial reporting and forecasting
- Cash flow management effectiveness
- Cost reduction and efficiency improvements
- Compliance with regulations
- Successful fundraising or IPO execution
- Budget variance management
- ROI on investments
- Audit results
- Investor satisfaction
